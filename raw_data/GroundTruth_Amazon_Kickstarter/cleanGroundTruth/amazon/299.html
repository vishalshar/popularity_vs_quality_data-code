5.0 out of 5 starsVery robust
ByDon A.on December 20, 2016
Color: black
I bought 2 Momas scooters after having owned different types of "last mile" transport. So I believe I have a good idea of what works and what works better. Of course everything has it's trade offs, but with these, I feel like I'm pretty well ahead of the game.

I am a 66 year old sailor. These scooters were bought to use as ground transport for my wife and myself when we arrive in different ports around the world. Too many of the places we go have less than perfect pavement. That is a very big reason I chose the Momas with the 10 inch pneumatic tires. The bigger the wheels, the more comfortable and stable the ride. But of course there is a point when you have to consider that you are not choosing a bicycle for a reason. For me, that reason is more important than most. A bicycle is just too bulky to store inside my floating home. I can easily fit 2 powered scooters in less space than one bicycle.

Security is another very important consideration to me. By that, I mean that these scooters should very rarely be out of my sight. There is no way that I would consider trying to lock these up to a bike rack, even if one was always available, while I go in to buy groceries. I put them on the rack on the bottom of the shopping cart and go inside. I did include the optional wheeled carrying bag with my purchase, which makes life easier when I need to go into some places, like my visit to the dentist. The two carry handles make good shoulder straps to carry the bag on your back while riding the scooter.

Other scooters I looked at had their positives of course. I simply feel that my research came up with Momas as the most robust, comfortable scooters for the best price. Then you throw in the features like the brake light and easily folding handlebars on top.

One feature that I would like to see for my own situation, is a way of locking the steering when folded. Due to the fact that the pivot point for folding is at the front of the deck as opposed to low on the steering column, the column can rotate. This can result in the electrical cable being stressed where the cable enters the deck, and the control/display module on the handlebars coming in contact with the rear fender, which could damage the module. I would certainly not make this a "deal killer", especially since this point on folding is very common on other units that I researched. My fix is a simple small bungee cord run through the front wheel and back around the pivot point on the deck.

I will say that among the first questions I always get, is "How fast will it go?" My reply always starts with "Fast enough to hurt you." Be careful. Be smart.
