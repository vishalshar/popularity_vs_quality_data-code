Product Description

Pauline Oliveros composer, performer, and
humanitarian is an important pioneer in American
Music. Internationally acclaimed, for four decades
she has explored sound, forging new ground for
herself and others. Through improvisation, electronic
music, ritual, teaching, and meditation she has
created a body of work with such breadth of vision
that it profoundly affects those who experience it,
and eludes many who try to write about it. Whether
performing at the JFK Center in Washington DC, in
an underground cavern, or in the studios of West
German Radio, Oliveros commitment to interaction
with the moment is unchanged.
Oliveros has built a loyal following through her
concerts, recordings, publications, and musical
compositions. She has provided leadership within
the music community, from her early years as
the first Director of the Center for Contemporary
Music (formerly the Tape Music Center at Mills)
and director of the Center for Music Experiment
during her 14-year tenure as professor of music
at UC San Diego to acting in an advisory capacity
for organizations such as the National Endowment
for the Arts, New York State Council for the Arts,
and many private foundations. She now serves
as Distinguished Research Professor of Music at
Rensselaer Polytechnic Institute and Darius Milhaud
Composer in Residence at Mills College.

5.0 out of 5 starsUtterly Fantastic
Bygrooveron September 16, 2012
Format: Audio CD
Something told me this was monumental even though I have unsuccessfully tried to "get" her music since the early 80's. I saw her live about 2 years ago and left glad but not thrilled that I went. Despite all that, I remained fascinated by one of her early works on Odyessy Records -the Music Of Our Time series- and thinking this might be more treasure like that, I snatched this up right away. It is not only thrilling, it is astounding. I would never have put Oliveros in the top 5 of all the "classical" electronic/tape composers I have heard, but now I must consider her numero uno. Stockhausen, Cage, Henry, Ligeti, the Princeton collective, and all the others had excellent moments, but I do not think anyone produced anything close to this. This body of work is consistently inventive, captivating, involving, and reveals a logic driven by deep emotion. Not one work here sounds like an after thought, a half baked idea, or filler. So much electronic composition - especially a lot of the early work - comes across as logical statements/ exercises in an idea/ an emotional challenge/ or a demonstration record. Heard once or twice and been there done that. Not so here. These are alluring, rich, emotional, personal novels to be savored and enjoyed many times. Given the dates of these works, I am all the more amazed as she simply leaves her contemporaries of the time in the dust with her originality. The evocative sounds, her fascinating composing, the tremendous breadth of ideas, the emotional involvement she generates left me playing as many of the cd's as I could one after another. I could not wait for the next even though pinned to the chair by the one I was hearing. Nonpariel.Timeless.
