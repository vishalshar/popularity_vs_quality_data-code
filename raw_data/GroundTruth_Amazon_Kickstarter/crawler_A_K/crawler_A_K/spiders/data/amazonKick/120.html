About the Startup

Describe your product in 3 words.

Young Creator's Dream!

How did you come up with the idea for this product?

Our 3D printer had almost completed a 14-hour print when it missed a line, leaving an unplanned gap in the print. A small error had ruined hours of work, and we were going to have to start again. Then inspiration hit: “what if we just took the head of the printer off and filled in the gap?” A day later the 3Doodler was born. The first 3Doodler was a clumsy-looking, hand-assembled, device, with the words “3Doodler” etched onto its side in green and red in felt tip pen. The 3Doodler Start was a natural progression for us, creating a kid-safe and friendly device specifically for young creators!

What makes your product special?

The 3Doodler Start was specifically designed for young creators who love to express themselves through the things they make. The 3Doodler Start is also the first 3D printing pen on the market to be truly kid-safe and friendly. There are no hot parts! Designed to be as simple to use as possible, kids can can pick up the 3Doodler Start and get creating within minutes. The 3Doodler Start works wirelessly for up to an hour, and can also be used with a range of 3Doodler accessories, including our themed DoodleBlocksTM - simple templates for super Doodles!

What has been the best part of your startup experience?

The best part has been building a vibrant and creative community! Starting with our first Kickstarter campaign, we have always put community front and center. Hundreds of thousands of units later, it's thrilling to see what our community has been doing with the 3Doodler, and how they have shared with and inspired each other (and us!). Their creations are all over social media sites and on our website, and extend from craft and design, through to innovative hacks and fixes, and into a never ending array of educational uses. With 3Doodler Start, that creativity and community will now embrace a whole new generation!

4.0 out of 5 starsBest 3D Pen for Kids, Very Expensive Proprietary Plastic
ByKindle CustomerTOP 500 REVIEWERVINE VOICEon September 11, 2016
Vine Customer Review of Free Product( What's this? )
My kids, 10 and 12, have been using this pen now for a week, after having used the Hatchbox 3D pen for several months HATCHBOX 1.75mm 3D Printing Pen with LCD Screen for ABS and PLA Filaments. They are now fighting over who gets to use the 3Doodler Start pen, for several reasons- according to the kids (in my words):

Pros of 3Doodler-Start Pen:
- internal usb re-chargeable battery; thus no need to have extra power cord sticking out the back and getting in the way
- cool tip, no need to be extra careful
- proprietary "Eco-Plastic" is softer after placing, and can be molded for a very short time by finger (cool enough and malleable enough to smooth out some surfaces, for example)
-plastic is cool enough to even use directly on the provided paper book stencils- the Eco plastic does not stick to the pages of the book, and comes right off the paper!
- less "threading" of filament when taking pen away from project
- less jamming (very critical), so no need to have backwards button to remove filament for cleaning- it appears to have an auto clearing feature that moves the filament backwards and forwards to unstick.
- one big ON button, and auto flow of filament, so you don't have to keep pressing the forward button to squeeze out plastic continuously as you work (slower flowrate, but they don't mind)
-The silicon green circular molds provided in this Mega set are really easy to use, have a lot of useful shapes, and the finished products pop right out.

Cons:
- you run out of filament quickly, because the provided strands are fairly short- about 6"- exactly the length of a US dollar bill, and you have to keep replacing them into the pen, causing delays. also, one larger project uses up an entire pack of filaments- my son could not complete his (not so large) helicopter project (from the book), for example, and needed to change colors after going through all the red (he did say he used some of the red for his Eiffel tower, however). My daughter pointed out that her one dinosaur used 1/2 the pack of whites.
-a negative to the melted Eco plastic not sticking on paper, is that it does not stick on paper!-- thus you can't use the paper to "anchor" a melted blob as easily, and the entire piece may lift up with the pen (but not a major problem)
-several examples of projects in the book use colors not provided in this Mega set, which my daughter found annoying.

This pen and Mega set are great, hands down, esp. for kids, and the proprietary "Eco" plastic is very beneficial and superior in the several aspects covered above, as well as being non-toxic. Also, there is almost no smell from the melted Eco plastic, vs. when my kids use the PLA or ABS filaments for the Hatchbox pen (I usually open a window). I deducted 1 star, because the cost for these great proprietary Eco plastic filaments is REALLY HIGH ($7.99 for one pack of 24-six inch pieces- 3Doodler Start Eco Plastic Pack: Lemon Zest (Lemon Yellow)).

The Eco filaments for this 3Doodler -Start are much thicker than the standard 1.75 mm used by a lot of 3D pens, and i don't want to try using incompatible sizes in the 3Doodler-Start pen. Interestingly, on the 3Doodler-Start Eco filament pack, it says that these Eco plastic filaments are not even compatible with other 3Doodle pens like the 3Doodler 1.0, 2.0, Create, or Pro versions. Perhaps this Eco-plastic filament pretty much only works with this one pen- the 3Doodler-Start.

Our Hatchbox pen, which uses the standard 1.75mm filaments that a lot of 3D pens (and 3D desk printers) use, has options to purchase giant rolls of filament weighing 1 Kg (not sure of length cuz they sell by weight). See pic where i have our our glow-in-the-dark filament roll- HATCHBOX 3D PLA-1KG1.75-GLOW PLA 3D Printer Filament, Dimensional Accuracy +/- 0.05 mm, 1 kg Spool, 1.75 mm, Glow in the Dark priced at $23.99 shown next to an Eco pack) for comparison. The pics below are projects my kids did from the book provided in this Mega set, except for the little green guy with antennae sitting next to the Eiffel Tower, which was previously made with PLA plastic and the Hatchbox pen.

I'd say this pen is a 5 star for ease of use, good Eco-plastic and convenience, but a 3 star for cost and inconvenience of short pieces of proprietary plastic.

5.0 out of 5 starsSo much fun for 7 year old artist!
Byjmwon December 25, 2016
Verified Purchase
We bought this for our 7 year old who loves to draw and doodle. I was a little nervous of the learning curve other's mention. But it's a short learning curve! My 7 year old picked it up quickly!! She still has some to learn - she moves the pen faster than the filament comes out. But she'll get more patience in time ;) Within 2 hours out of the box, she'd already made glasses! (photo attached)

This came with a full charge (I was pleasantly surprised!) and an idea book that has templates in it, on thick excellent quality paper. It's a great way to start!! And there's a bunch of silicone molds she has fun with, too! The idea book tells you how many filament strips each design takes - we found that's not accurate. The glasses should have taken 4 strands and really took her 7. It may be just because she's still learning.

It is not hot to the touch at all. Which is great, because it's harder to "cut" a line of plastic when you're done. Pushing it off the end with your finger works best (so far). And there's zero fear of burning yourself!

My only wish is that I would love it to have a speed setting. The plastic comes out very slowly. Which is good for little kids as they trace a template. But when you're just filling in the lines to make the design thicker or connecting two pieces together, it would be nice to double the speed and go faster. All said though, it's a great toy that our 7 year old will love for weeks, months, and probably years to come!

The charging cord is the same style as the kindle fire - which is great. The fewer cords we need at our ever growing family charging station, the better ;)
