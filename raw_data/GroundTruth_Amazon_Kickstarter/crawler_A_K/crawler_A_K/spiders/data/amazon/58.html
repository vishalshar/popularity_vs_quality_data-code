2.0 out of 5 starsDon't bother. Get Valkyria Chronicles Remastered instead.
Byfoldedsocksninjaon June 9, 2016
Platform: PlayStation 4|Verified Purchase
This could have been a decent turn based strategy title for the PS4, but crippled by some seriously poor design choices and oversights it becomes an exercise in tedium. The basic game mechanics follow games like Advance Wars, or Force Commander on the PSP. But don't let that fool you into thinking this game is worth your time.

Here are the main points that drive me nuts about this game:
- You can use L1 and R1 to cycle between your units that still have unused actions. Convenient, right? Wrong. Let's say you press R1 a bunch of times to get to a unit you want to move, and then move it, pressing R1 again doesn't go to the next unit. It goes back to the FIRST unit. EVERY TIME. It also includes units that have no moves left but do have an attack action, even though no enemy units are within range. Useless.

- Units can pass through cells occupied by your other units, but not by enemy units. Makes sense. Except units from allied factions also block yours making for some very frustrating situations. Oh, also aircraft are blocked by enemy ground units. They can pass obstacles and trees just fine, but that enemy soldier? Forget about it.

- The resource system in this game is just atrocious. Instead of having a global pool of resources (which are cleverly called "resources" in game), each building has it's own pool. Not such a problem on its own, except that each depot or factory will only have enough resources to build or repair at most 4 units. Occasionally you get a building that gets +5 resources per turn, but this is very rare. This means you often waste turn upon turn shuffling resources between buildings using a transport just to repair a unit, and end up with a bunch of units near death with no way to heal them once resources run out. This is probably the developers' definition of "challenge" but it's just cheap and annoying.

- Ammo is also in short supply for units that use it, same deal as resources.

- Units can level up to unlock perks which is nice, or you could use your level up to repair 40HP to your unit. Doesn't matter what unit or how many HP it has in total, you can only repair 40. And since the game has such limited supply of resources, you almost always end up using a unit's level up to heal rather than add a perk. And since they can only level up twice, the perks are gone forever.

- Aircraft are basically useless. To use them (except for gliders and helicopters) you have to deploy them from an airbase or carrier. This means you send them out to the tile next to an enemy but they can't move or attack until next turn. Of course the enemy either moves out of range or shoots down your plane before it does anything. If you try to chase them (even for just one turn) you can end up without enough fuel to get back to the base and your plane crashes. So don't bother with them.

- The controls! Too many touch screen-isms that are just unnecessary with a PS4 controller.

So many aspects of this game are just so annoying it sucks any enjoyment there might be had. Each mission can take hours to finish, but it's not time well spent. I actually finished the game because I'm a sucker and all but one mission I finished all secondary goals without reinforcements on the first attempt. So much for challenge. The whole time the credits were rolling I just kept wondering how so many people can be involved in a game that gets so many simple things wrong. If the developers released a major update to fix and refine it this could be an OK game, but I doubt they will so just go play something else. Like Valkyria Chronicles Remastered if you're looking for some turn-based strategy.

3.0 out of 5 starsIt's ok, but ok isn't enough to be worth your time
ByMichaelTOP 500 REVIEWERVINE VOICEon May 29, 2016
Platform: PlayStation 4
It's nowhere near the caliber of a good turn based strategy game, but it's one of the few turn bases strategy games for the ps4. I think it's interesting, but I'm not sure it's $20 interesting. I think budget titles are really hurt by the fact that blockbuster games eventually become budget titles. So we expect a lot more for even $20.

I thought it was going to be real-time strategy. It really looks like a Command and Conquer style game. But it's turn based strategy on a hex map. There is a decent variety of vehicles, but every side has the same exact vehicles just different colors. Fog of war plays a big part of the game. But really it means you can't really plan ahead and you often don't know how long the mission will be.

At first I thought it was hard, but I think it was because I was trying to play all bravado. I would have multiple battles going at once in different directions. I would attack guys here there and everywhere all at the same time. Well, that didn't work well. Everywhere I attacked, I'd get overwhelmed and crushed. You can treat the game as a puzzle. You can keep going at it over and over looking for the one way that you can attack with perfect strategy--sending guys here and there and everywhere and having a full scale war going on. Doing that, you'll have to try over and over and over again. What I found is that the enemy AI is very reactive. It doesn't attack you till you get right in it's face. So instead of having a multi-front war, stay in a pack and take things one battle at a time. Even when it seems like the game wants to split you up, don't do it! If you can, get the bad guys to attack you on a narrow path.

Taking things one step at a time allows you to actually get through the game without getting super frustrated with the amount of times you have to start a level over. But it makes the game quite tedious. Maybe if you're an awesome strategy player you can find ways to go win in a blaze of glory, but I couldn't figure out how to do better than one little fight, then another little fight, and yet another little fight. You also get to keep your guys from level to level and you can level them up too. There's a real benefit to keeping your guys alive--which again is another reason not to spread them out where you can't protect them.

It's fun to take over factories and get more units, but since there's no way to move more than one person at a time, it means the latter end of a battle becomes a slog of moving a guy, attacking, moving a guy attacking, moving a guy back to a repair spot, moving another guy in and attacking. You can pack some guys into a carrier, but the packing and unpacking takes time too.

In the end, I played about half way through before deciding this is not worth my time. It's not the most exciting. If you want a sci-fi ps4 turn based game, well you don't have many options and this may fit your bill. But there are a number of better games. If you want better story and a difficulty that needs real strategy, I suggest the Banner Saga. Valkyria Chronicles Remastered has a challenge, but is more approachable--and it's mixes in parts of a third person action game in a way that still works for a turn based game. Then if you like customizing the way your customization customizes the game, Disgaea 5 is the strategy game for you! I think any one of these is a better game than Battle Worlds: Kronos. It makes me hope that Xcom 2 will come to the PS4.
