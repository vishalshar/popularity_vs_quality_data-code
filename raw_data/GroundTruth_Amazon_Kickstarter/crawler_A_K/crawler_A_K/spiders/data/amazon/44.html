5.0 out of 5 starsJust... wow
ByM. Wethon February 9, 2015
Format: Audio CD|Verified Purchase
Wow. I just don't have enough good things to say. Highlights include:

- Big Dark Love. What a fantastic title track. It's mellow, saucy, dirty, and just sounds huge. It's pretty amazing when a track can just take your mind to another place...

- Which obviously leads to Dream In Red. This track leaves me speechless. I like to listen to it a few times in a row. It's like some wild, surreal alternate reality. Crazy how good this one is.

- Send Me Home is not only my favorite track on the album, it's one of the best songs I've ever heard. The despair is palpable, yet there's a certain joy to it. Really tugs at the heart-strings. Simply stunning.

- The bass in The Last Thing is delicious. Heavy, clean, and nice and bouncy. I want to eat it with a knife and fork. Sometimes I can hardly hear the rest of the track over the sheer awesomeness of that bass...

- Hunted? Yeah, that cello is hunting my soul, and it hurts so good. Dammit, another amazing track.

I agree with Aja, I wasn't sure at first. Now I'm sure. I'm sure that I like it more and more every time I hear it. Just buy this album.

3.0 out of 5 starsGood, not their best.
ByMatthew Hamannon April 25, 2015
Format: MP3 Music|Verified Purchase
I love Murder by Death and this is good but not their best. If you've never heard Murder By Death, their third, fourth and fifth albums are what you should listen to first. The first song on this album - "I Shot An Arrow" - sounds like something designed for American radio play and it is pretty awful. The rest of the album ranges from decent to enjoyable. I especially liked the tracks "It Will Never Die," "Natural Pearl," and "Big Dark Love." In some cases the album sounds more accessible to the masses, in other cases it sounds more country. I do like hearing MBD's growth and for fans of them it is worth checking out.
