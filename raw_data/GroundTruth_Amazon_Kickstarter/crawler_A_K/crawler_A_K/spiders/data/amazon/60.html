Designed By Creatives, For Creatives

As designers, we know how important our tools are to our work. That's why we've created the perfect notebook to help us share and record all of our ideas in a clean, elegant way. The left pages in our notebook are marked with a dot grid, acting as guidelines to bring our designs to life. The right pages are printed with our signature Indentation Rule, giving you a subtle outline to easily align your notes, whether it be for lists, paragraphs, or even code. Your notes are important. The Traveler helps make them beautiful.


Dot Grid & Indentation Rule

Left side pages contain a dot grid for drawings, sketches, and wireframes. Right side contains our Indentation Rule for beautiful notes, lists, paragraphs, and code.


Thick, Durable Pages

Lay Flat Design

We use a premium binding that ensures every notebook lays flat, no matter what page you are on. The perfect writing environment, every time.


The Notebook, Done Right.

At Code&Quill, we believe that quality is everything. Your notebook carries your most cherished thoughts and ideas, and they deserve a home that will treat them as such. The Traveler is the perfect combination of sophistication and simplicity; everything you need from a notebook, and nothing that you don’t.

About the Startup

Describe your product in 3 words.

Notebooks for creatives.

How did you come up with the idea for this product?

When I had started a tech company a few years back, I spent a lot of time co-developing our enterprise product with our lead web engineer. We constantly were going over design mockups, algorithm designs, and wireframe sketches in various notebooks. There was a point at which I grew frustrated with how quickly pages would fall out and tear, and that there wasn’t anything out there that was specifically catered to the types of creative work I was doing in terms of the way the pages themselves were laid out. I looked for quite a while to see if any of the options out there today had a solution, but realized that there weren’t any. I decided that if it wasn’t available, then I might as well see if other people were having the same problems I was, and if they’d be interested in a new type of notebook that solved those issues. It turns out that there were plenty of people out there who were experiencing the same frustrations that I was. Code&Quill was born shortly after.

What makes your product special?

There are a handful of characteristics that separate our notebooks from others. First, our notebooks all lay-flat, meaning that the book won’t close in on you as you’re writing, and the pages don’t try to flip over, regardless of which side you are working on. Second, our pages are made with thick, 100GSM acid-free paper, which is to say that this isn’t the type of paper you’ll find in standard notebooks. They are meant to be more durable and won’t tear easily. Third, our unique page layout really focuses on the way we know a lot of people work, and provides some structure to your notes, without getting in your way. Many popular notebooks brands have always seen their products as temporary and disposable. We believe that your notebook should be a timeless archive that holds the inner workings of your mind during some period in your life.

What has been the best part of your startup experience?

The best part, by far, has been the feedback that we receive from customers. There is no better feeling in the world than receiving an unexpected email or call from one of our awesome users about how our products exceeded their expectations, or that their friends are all wondering where they can get one of their own Code&Quill notebooks. To have something that you’ve created be positively received by people you’ve never met is phenomenal.

4.0 out of 5 starsIt's Good, But Not Perfect.
ByJoe Hammon February 19, 2017
Color: White|Verified Purchase
What it is:
This is a notebook/journal/sketchbook by the company code&quill. For what it is, it's fairly unique. Unlike most typical notebooks, this one is outfitted with a dot-grid design on the left page and indented line on the right page. While this is a obviously a multi-use design, its intended to be mainly used for coding, architecture, and things of that nature. Besides that, it's exactly what it looks like. I'd label some more technical specs, but I feel that the description is actually well in-depth.

Appearance:
I do not know how I feel about this notebook's appearance. It is very minimalistic, which I am personally a fan of. There is nothing big or flashy on it besides possibly the worst design choice of the entire product: the red patch. Frankly, and in my opinion, it is not a pretty patch to begin with. First, the patch on mine is crookedly made. This wouldn't be such a big deal if the cover had anything else to draw attention to, but it lacks that. Second, the patch has slightly crooked stitching around the borders. Third, the "code&quill" is not stitched, but rather heat-applied to the patch (by the look of it; I'm not an expert). And, finally, the patch is glued on, as many have stated. Before you think about just removing it, it is glued on in a way that makes removal very possible, but very ugly when done. But I'll get more into that later. Besides that, there are a few nitpicks I have, such as the cheap looking cover and the cardboard spine. However, the cover is actually good quality and just looks cheap, and, well...the spine is just annoying. Everything in the entire book is either white (like the cover), grey (like the lines), or red. But the cardboard is very much cardboard brown. It just looks off.

Mechanics:
Mechanically speaking, this product is fairly well done. I have had no problems with the quality of the binding, the paper is great to write on (perhaps more so with a pen rather than a pencil), and the cover bends without creasing for the most part. It even works on solving the age-old journal problem of not being able to write on the inner 10% of the page, which is somewhat fixes. The real mechanical failings of this notebook is, a.) the very light grey lines, b.) , the indents on the indented rule side being too small, and c.) the stupid red patch. First, the grey lines are way too light, causing it to be flat out hard to see at times. Most lines are either nearly black or are a different color to make quick differentiating easy, but these are light grey. It is very annoying when trying to quickly grasp where an indention or where another dot on the dot graph is. Second, the indents on the indented rule are just tiny. They are the size of one letter or number. I have no idea on whether this is intentional or not, but the indents serve no purpose for me due to this. Lastly, that dumb red patch is mechanically terrible. It has the likely possibility of getting caught on everything, it is not glued on super well, and I am almost certain the glue would get stuck to the cover should the patch be ripped off. As it is, I fight for dear life trying to put this notebook in places where nothing will catch that dumb red patch. I know, it sounds like I'm overstating the red patch problem, but a stupid patch should not have such weight in my mind as to where I will store the journal safely. It is utterly annoying and pointless.

Personal Notes:
I got this notebook for Dungeons and Dragons. With the left page being a grid and the right side being lined, I could easily make this a one stop notebook for holding dungeons. Maps on the left, dungeon notes and content on the right. And, for this purpose, the notebook has pulled through tremendously. It cut down on my carrying two full sized notebooks all the time (something that is important for a traveling Dungeon Master), and it has simplified my life while creating dungeons. Maybe this product is better for a coder, or maybe it is worse. Whatever the case, my review is based on my experiences.

Overall:
This little notebook has a lot of things going for it, from the nice overall design to the innovative inner design. However, it isn't perfect, with many small things that keep this product from being truly great--such as the red patch of doom, the showing cardboard, and the deceptive looking cover material. Overall, I like this product. I feel like it is very close to being what I want it to be, but it just is not quite there. I hope, should code&quill make a new softcover, indentation ruled notebook, they take this into consideration.
