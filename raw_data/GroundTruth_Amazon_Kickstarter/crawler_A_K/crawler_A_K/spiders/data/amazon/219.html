About the Startup

Describe your product in 3 words.

Simple, Bright, Smart

Where did you get the inspiration for your product?

We had our lightbulb moment back in 2010 while Cofounders Corey Egan & Swapnil Bora were MBA classmates. We saw what was happening with LED lighting and mobility and realized that there was huge potential to improve and enhance how we interact with light on a daily basis. So we submitted an idea for ilumi into a school competition and won. From there we went on to crowdfunding, Shark Tank, and have now shipped tens of thousands of ilumi Smartbulbs across the world.

What differentiates your product from similar products out there?

When we designed ilumi, we designed it around the customer. The result of that is ilumi brighter, simpler, and smarter than any other light bulb and delivers an experience that puts smiles on people's faces.

Tell us about the best and most challenging parts of the creation process.

The most amazing and difficult part of a creation process is making decisions about what's going to be important to the customer and accurately predicting what they want and being able to deliver it. It really requires a tremendous amount of observation, listening, and empathy. For ilumi, we see that people really can perceive what good lighting is if they see it, but have very little tools and knowledge to be able to achieve good lighting. The process of creating a product that helps people achieve better lighting and being part of people's homes is the most rewarding part.

4.0 out of 5 starsWish it would remember the brightness I had after using the light switch...
ByCarinaVINE VOICEon April 28, 2015
Color: Arctic White|Vine Customer Review of Free Product( What's this? )
Like others have said, there really are no instruction with this light other than on the box saying Screw in, Download App, Pair and enjoy. Once you do install the app it does have a tutorial that goes through and points out how to use some of the features on the screen. And you can reset the tutorial to go back and do it again in case you forgot how to do something.

I installed this on my Samsung S5 with no problem. Before it was paired up with my phone though it the light would cycle through colors until it was paired with the phone and I picked what light I wanted it to be. The app is pretty easy to use, it wants to group the light first and it defaults to kitchen. I put it in my living room. The light is very bright and natural white light. It is brighter than most of the lights in my house.

In the control bar in the app there is Groups, Experiences, scenes, add ilumi, account and support. The Experience setting is the funniest. It has Rise & shine, Music sync, Scene scheduler, vacation, circadian, torch and simon. Most of those are self-explanatory. Scenes from what I can tell is you select a color you like and then press the camera button and it with capture that so you can go back to it. And then you can choose certain times of the day that that scene can come one. You can also names those scenes to whatever you want as well.

The app is okay, pretty user-friendly but some things are very intuitive. And the first time I was playing with it the app force stop and I had to reopen it. Some of the features are a little finicky and it wouldn't change when I wanted it to. Or in the app it would show the brightness at the lowest but then the light switch was turn off and on and the light was definitely not at what it was showing on the app. Or it would take awhile to register the color change or not change at all.

What I don't like about this light is, if you use the light switch to turn it off and then turn it back on it doesn't remember the setting you had it at. And it just goes back to default brightness everytime. I don't like this because it is natural to turn on and off the light switch, so if you accidently turn it off and then back on it messing up what I had programmed into it. The default brightness is too bright for me. It also doesn't dim that low, I like to have a dim light in the morning when I am rocking my baby or nursing her back to sleep and this light even at it's dimmest setting is still too bright. But my 2 year gets a kick out of it.

***Update***
I found out that you can change what the light setting for the light switch is. On the color wheel screen in the upper righthand corner it says default. At first, I thought default meant change all the settings back to factory default because that is what default normally means. But not in this case. For this default, you pick your color and your brightness and then press default and now that is what the light defaults to when you turn on and off your light switch which I found pretty nifty.

I guess it is kind of a learning game with this smartbulb since there really isn't any instructions just the basic tutorial that doesn't go over everything. But it is fun exploring it and learning new things.

2.0 out of 5 starsPoor Software Experience
Byheri16on September 20, 2015
Color: Arctic White
The concept is great but the execution is poor. I got a unit during the Kickstarter fundraising campaign. Was excited by their promise of great lumens output across a temperature/color range and detection of presence via Bluetooth Low Energy. When the product was delivered, it was easy to setup, but the experiences that were promised such as circadian lighting was horribly implemented with no thought for the end user. The color temperature was an eerie red during 5 am when it should have been a blue color to induce energetic state of mind. At night, it was a sunset orange but it was too bright and overpowering even late into the night. Soon I discovered that there were no options to tune the circadian cycle and the brightness settings for the circadian mode, and even after turning it down manually to comfort level via the app, every hour the bulb will have a mind of its own and go back into full brightness every hour.

With this I begin to doubt that the app developers even eat their own dog food, to have delivered such flawed logic and reasoning for the user experience. The Bluetooth Low Energy presence detection for automatic light-on was finicky as the app didn't stay active in the background. Having been a mobile software developer myself a few years back, I discovered that the right APIs where not used to implement the solution properly. They should have used iBeacons when on iOS platform and background headless services, and cellular geofencing when on Android. Because of the lack of know-how to use the right technology for the problem at hand, you can never rely on this smart light detecting your presence in each room reliably. I think the success rate is about 50:50, which is way too low even for a consumer product.

My brother uses Philips Hue bulbs, a likely competitor to the iLumi, and through my testing, it has much better software. The iLumi might sound great on paper, even in videos, but during real-life use, it is an utter letdown due to the flawed software design.
