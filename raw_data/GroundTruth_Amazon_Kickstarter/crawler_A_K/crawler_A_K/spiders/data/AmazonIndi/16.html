About the Startup

Describe your product in 3 words.

Fun Interactive Toothbrush

How did you come up with the idea for this product?

When Dr. Wang's son Charles was 8 years old, he used to HATE to brush his teeth. The result was he had to have several teeth restored. Dr. Wang thought there had to be a batter way! This is why he invented Grush. He solved the problem for his familiy first then teamed up with Dr. Anubha and Ethan to make his invention availible for parents and kids all around the world.

What makes your product special?

Grush has developed tooth-to-tooth navagation techonology that transforms brushing into fun and interactive games.

What has been the best part of your startup experience?

We have received enthusiastic support from Dentists, Parents, and Kids, all around the world. One Mother told us that "Grush is finally bringing toothbrushing into the 21st century"

3.0 out of 5 starsGood Idea, poor implementation
ByAjay R. Yadavon January 22, 2017
Verified Purchase
There are a lot of positive reviews on this product so I decided to buy it. Grush is a really cool idea but very poor implementation. I have designed and develop bluetooth apps that work with hardware, I can think of many tweaks I can make that would make it work better.
1) 6-Year old does not have enough time to get the monster. When she does, the grush does not register so she has to move her mouth to get it. (calibration issues) There is no way to calibrate the Grush via the application.
2) you have to be right handed, yes it mentions this in the manual that you HAVE to start with right hand and brush head right in the middle, but my daughter has her right arm in a cast due to elbow injury, so she cannot use it, she does not understand so now her calibration is all off.
3) Bluetooth connection is really unreliable with ipad. Some time it connects and some time it doesn't. I have to go through whole routine to take the battery out, put it back in, let it sit until the light turns off on grush. Toggle bluetooth on ipad and then it connects. A 6 year old would not know how to do this.
4) I have no idea how The parent companion app works. It had no user interface to let you know that you have to connect to the existing Grush and if there are multiple Grush then you wouldn't know which one is connected and which one isn't. We have used it over a month but it still tells me that neither one of my kids have brush their teeth EVER.
