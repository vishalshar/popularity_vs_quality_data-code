About the Startup

Describe your product in 3 words.

Simple. Audiophile. Beautiful.

Where did you get the inspiration for your product?

When Olive was originally founded in 2005 the way people listened to music was fundamentally different to today. Music is now spread over a whole range of devices (Smartphones, PC, Mac, NAS etc.) and available from a wide variety of music streaming services (Pandora, Spotify etc.). The Olive ONE was designed to bring all these devices and services together so you can control all your music from one simple (and gorgeous) touch-screen interface. And stream it wirelessly throughout your home.

What differentiates your product from similar products out there?

The Olive ONE is basically a “high-end SONOS system” and differentiates itself against its competitors in 3 areas: 1) Sound quality: The ONE comes with a high-end BurrBrown 32-bit/384kHz DAC and also plays HD music. It also features Dual HD amplifiers (2x 32W @ 8 Ohm) to drive passive speakers; 2) Independence: We have integrated a LCD and a Hard Disk Drive into the ONE. This allows the ONE to operate independently from the network and can be easily controlled at all times (without requiring you get out your smartphone and launching an app); 3) Design: The ONE is built out of fine-grade aluminum and glass materials.

Tell us about the best and most challenging parts of the creation process.

Changing from a traditional to a crowd-driven product development process was at the same time the biggest challenge and best part of the creation process. We decided to use a crowd-funding platform to launch the Olive ONE because we wanted to extend our horizon and tap into the immense knowledge and dedication of music fans and enthusiasts all around the world. The ONE is the direct result of the feedback we received from thousands of supporters and we are continuously implementing new feedback every day.

3.0 out of 5 stars(Almost) a Killer App
Bydeutscherhififanon June 13, 2015
Size: 0TB
I just received my unit fresh from Taiwan yesterday. Given some of the negative reviews I was a little anxious, but after unpacking and going through a few simple set up steps to link it to my network and NAS I realized there was nothing to worry about. The interface is one of the best I have used, the firmware responds very quickly and music plays instantly, even given my large collection on the NAS. It is too early to judge sound quality, but from what I hear cold out of the box I am very intrigued. As a reference point, my main system uses only cables that are more expensive that the Olive One. For the money, this little guy is amazing and made me think I should at some point get rid of my mono tube amps, preamp, DAC, etc. and just get an efficient pair of speakers and ride into the sunset using the Olive One. Until that happens, it will serve double duty and drive outdoor speakers when needed or provide music in our basement playroom. Oh, I forgot to mention that the iPad app also works great. One tip: there is a setting for which antenna you want to use: internal or external. I did not know that and was dismayed that the One could not connect to my network from my outdoor position. However, after changing the setting to external antenna (I had already installed it before) there were no connection issues. Second tip: review the firmware upgrade roadmap on the Olive site. There is a major new release planned every few months with DSD also on the horizon and some great new functionality coming. Very highly recommended.

Update after 5 months:

There are definitely a few bugs that have to be fixed and the promised integration of music services such as spotify has still not happened. I ended up putting in a hard drive since streaming wasn't reliable over (admittedly) a fairly long distance. The unit runs VERY hot after just an hour of playing which seems to negatively impact touch input processing response times. Sadly, I had to downgrade to 3 stars. It is ok for my intended use though (powering speakers on the side of the garage which fire into the backyard).

2.0 out of 5 starsA long way from ready
ByEvil Overlordon November 4, 2015
Size: 1TB
I've had my Olive One for over a year now. The summary: I replaced it with a HiFiBerry that does everything better.

A year and a half ago, I was looking for an alternative to the discontinued Logitech Touch. The then-still-forthcoming Olive One seemed to have everything I was looking for, and more. In particular, I wanted high quality sound, ability to play FLACs, play from NAS, on-board storage, and a nice touch-screen interface. The One promised all that, and more.

1. Sound quality - the sound quality is excellent.
2. FLAC - It plays FLACs with no trouble.
3. NAS play - not really, and many interface components don't work properly with remote storage.
4. Storage - I eventually installed my own hard drive. Transfer to-from the drive is not so easy, but it's possible.
5. Interface - here's where it all falls apart. The interface _looks_ nice, but it simply doesn't work. The screen is laggy and inconsistent. The software very often doesn't do what it's supposed to - taking me to screens I don't want, or refusing to act at all. As noted, some of the interface components (e.g., quick scroll by alphabet) only work properly with on-board storage.
WiFi connectivity is a crapshoot. Olive offers an Android App, which for a time sort of worked, then could only find (and thus control) the One some of the time, and finally lost its ability to find the One at all. I've uninstalled, reinstalled, etc., and the thing just doesn't work.

This was meant to be the heart of my sound system. I finally couldn't take it anymore, and replaced this $550 purchase with a $60 Raspberry PI with a HiFiBerry DAC, a nice case, and RuneAudio. It doesn't have a touch screen, but in every other way, it beats the pants off the One, and sounds just as good. I handed the One off to a relative who only plays music occasionally. I check in on it frequently, and it hasn't improved in any meaningful way.

The One was an expensive and very disappointing failed investment on my part, and I can't recommend buying one. It looks good, and it sounds good when you can get it to play. Sadly, that's not often.
